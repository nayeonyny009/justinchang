Process

Bear Arms

Designing a story-driven ecommerce experience that balances brand message, shopping clarity, and end-to-end product flow.

Overview

Bear Arms is a concept-driven ecommerce website that explores how a clothing brand can carry a social message without losing clarity as a shopping experience. The project began with a small idea: a website revealing the double meaning of “bear arms” and connecting it to gun violence awareness through one clothing item. It later expanded into a full ecommerce experience with collection, product detail pages, cart, checkout, and confirmation.

My Role

UX Designer — Website Structure, User Flow, Wireframing, Prototyping, Design System, Figma Refinement

I led most of the UX and website design work for Bear Arms, turning an early concept-focused prototype into a fuller ecommerce experience. I was responsible for the homepage structure, collection flow, product detail pages, cart, checkout, and order confirmation, while also building the wireframes, prototype, and key Figma system pieces that held the experience together.

Challenge

Balancing Message and Usability

The core challenge was balancing message and usability. The concept relied on a pun and socially charged idea, so the site couldn’t act like a standard clothing store. At the same time, it had to remain clear and believable.

This tension shaped design decisions: the entry point needed to reveal meaning without overwhelming, and interface elements had to maintain clarity.

Flow & IA

End-to-End Shopping Journey

Designed the user flow: landing → collection → product pages → cart → checkout → confirmation. Balanced narrative and usability to reveal the brand’s message progressively.

Scope Evolution

From Concept Reveal to Full Ecommerce Prototype

This section shows how the project expanded from an early concept-focused prototype into a fuller ecommerce experience. The first version centered on a smaller structure: a landing page, collection page, product detail pages, and message-sharing interactions. It helped communicate the brand idea, but the overall experience still felt limited as a complete shopping product.

As the project developed, I expanded the flow into a more complete ecommerce system. The later prototype added more product states, cart, checkout, and order confirmation, turning the project from a symbolic concept reveal into a connected shopping journey.

This shift was important because it made the project feel more believable as a digital product. Instead of only explaining the meaning of Bear Arms, the final structure gave users a clearer path from brand introduction to browsing, product selection, and purchase completion.

Figma Production

Prototyping and Design System

Figma was used for low/high-fidelity wireframes, prototype linking, and design system components/variants.

Usability Feedback

What Users Revealed

Evaluated prototype with first-time users. Main insights: entry clarity, action emphasis, and interaction cues.

Iteration

Refining Entry & Product Flow

Refined navigation, clarified labels, and improved product page interactions based on feedback.

AI Use

AI Support and Human Judgment

AI supported visualization for product images, but core UX, structure, and interface design were done by hand.

Final Outcome

Complete Ecommerce Experience

Full ecommerce prototype, complete product browsing, checkout, and confirmation flow. Cohesive brand storytelling and shopping experience.
